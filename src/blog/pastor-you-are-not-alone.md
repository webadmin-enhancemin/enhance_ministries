To those of you who read this, and to all the non-pastors who may come across it as well, I want to be very clear on something from the outset. What I have written here is 0% political and 100% pastoral. \
\
While I certainly have my opinions, I am not a banner-waver or crusader for either side. So if you read this through the lens of your viewpoint and either cheer me on as an ally or revile me as your adversary, you've missed my point. I am on the side of Jesus and the mission He left for His followers individually as well as the ***Big "C"*** Church together. 

In my role caring for and coaching pastors and ministry leaders as well as working with their churches and ministries as a whole I am deeply aware that you are in the midst of another concerning leadership and shepherding challenge. Doesn’t covid feel like a long time ago?! This message is for you to encourage you and exhort you to keep your eye on the big, Kingdom picture. 

I know I am not alone in the feelings of outrage and despair over what is happening in our world, country and specifically here where I live in the Twin Cities. I have seen Jesus loving people act and react on both extremes of the two sides of the conflict we're seeing splattered all over the news and social media. Pastor,I know you know this, but in a heart of encouragement and to remind you that ***you*** have the responsibility, not to take a stand on either side, but to shepherd your people to think, act, speak and react Biblically. 

While you may not want to talk about current or future issues right away, and there is certainly wisdom in not speaking without being educated, the reality is that your people *are* talking! Unfortunately we/you do not have the luxury of time when it comes to shepherding. While you may be waiting (please be encouraged by this) your people are scrolling social media, commenting, posting and having conversations with their neighbors, coworkers and anyone else who comes across their path. This may be speculation, but my sense is that most of those conversations are not moving them or your church closer to fulfilling the mission that Jesus left for us. We love our people, but what do you think? Are you confident that their conduct is Christ-like when it comes to interactions regarding current events? Note that I’m not talking about their opinions. Regardless of what they think, are they adorning the Gospel? 

Pastor, I know you know this, but still, I boldly exhort you in the Holy Spirit, to courageously shepherd your flocks to think and act rightly before the Lord in such a way that makes people, fellow followers of Jesus or pre-Christians, curious about the Gospel’s impact on all aspects of their lives. This will likely be well before you have your mind wrapped around any specific issue! Such a place of deep tension pastors find themselves in! How to shepherd a congregation of people while still on your own journey with the Lord through each day.

Again, I’m with you.

So with that said, I’ve got 5 items I would offer as the core communication and shepherding points you could/should share with your people. Use your own words, but I pray that what I feel the Lord has stirred in me here will serve you as you shepherd the followers of Jesus in your churches to shift their gaze away from the chaos all around us (key tool of the enemy) and back onto the mission God has for us individually and as the Church.  

## Be Biblically Grounded

I have seen far too many people whose passions, attitudes, words and actions are clearly more informed by what they are reading in their algorithm-fueled echo chambers online and unfortunately, based on their fruit, not on what Scripture says. Over the years I've used the analogy of a steak marinating in a delicious sauce to describe how Scripture needs to affect our lives. Once that steak is grilled up, no matter where you cut into it, it tastes like that marinade! *That* is how our and our peoples’ lives need to be. We need to marinate in God's Word so much so that wherever the world or circumstances 'cut' into us it is God's Word that flows out! This isn’t cheesy Sunday School stuff here, this is real life!

> "For out of the overflow of the heart, the mouth speaks* (and the life acts) (my emphasis added)"
> — Matthew 12:34 & Luke 6:45

Furthermore, being founded on and guided by Scripture is the only way through this journey of life.

> "Your Word is a lamp unto my feet and a light unto >my path."
> —Psalm 119:105
>
> "All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness, so that the servant of God\[a] may be thoroughly equipped for every good work."
> —2 Timothy 3:16-17

Don’t forget, what was Jesus’ response to temptation? Quoting Scripture! (Matthew 4 & Luke 4)

I pray you and I, and the churches we are a part of are so hungry for God's Word that we are filled with it 

> Man does not live on bread alone, but on every word that flows from the mouth of God*) to the point where we are able to correctly discern in each moment what is and is not aligned with it. 
> —Matthew 4:4

Pastor, I pray you are doing well in this area, well beyond sermon prep. Then, are your people doing well in this? Is your leadership, teaching and shepherding growing their Biblical literacy, their ability to feed themselves, to discern what is not Biblical in the world around them and the conviction to act on it?

## Be Theologically Driven

For your people, hearing or knowing God's Word is not enough. It must drive every thought, attitude, word, action and reaction each and every moment of every day of our lives. James 1:22 says, *"Do not be merely hearers of the Word, and so deceive yourselves. Do what it says!"* Our lives must be built upon truth from God's Word and then our actions flow out of that.

We cannot and must not think and act like the world does! The passage I quote every time I do a microphone check before a speaking engagement:

> "I urge you brothers and sisters, in view of God's mercy, offer your bodies as living sacrifices, holy and pleasing to God. This is your spiritual act of worship. Do not conform any longer to the pattern of this world but be transformed by the renewing of your minds. Then you will be able to test and approve what God's will is - His good, pleasing and perfect will."
> —Romans 12:1-2

Unfortunately if we throw our tube in the "Lazy River" (More to come on that soon!) of life and even church life and allow our minds to be filled with whatever voice is yelling the loudest, almost never the Holy Spirit (see 1 Kings 19), we will be conformed to those things rather than Scripture. 

May we be like the tree planted by streams of water we read about in Psalm 1:

> Blessed is the one who does not walk in step with the wicked or stand in the way that sinners take or sit in the company of mockers, but whose delight is in the law of the Lord, and who meditates on his law day and night. That person is like a tree planted by streams of water, which yields its fruit in season and whose leaf does not wither—whatever they do prospers. Not so the wicked! They are like chaff that the wind blows away
>
> Therefore the wicked will not stand in the judgment, nor sinners in the assembly of the righteous. For the Lord watches over the way of the righteous, but the way of the wicked leads to destruction.

While this point is probably going to be pretty universally accepted by all sides of the spectrum, my challenge to be theologically driven has less to do with the stance taken and more to do with how it is taken and communicated. 

Could a person be Biblically aligned in their beliefs but still be dead wrong? 

If how those beliefs play out in life with others, especially those who don’t know Jesus, is in a way that lacks the fruits of the Spirit (Galatians 5:22), then yes, absolutely, they are acting wrong. 

Our theology must drive our beliefs and actions. 

## Take the posture of a curious learner

This point is about as simply practical as I can get. Our society has become far too much about adversarially labeling those we disagree with and then taking the “moral high ground” and verbally, and unfortunately sometimes physically, assaulting them with our opinions and beliefs. Wouldn't the world be a better place if the very most passionate people about any particular viewpoint took the posture of a curious learner and started with asking questions versus lobbing unhelpful words or more over the metaphorical wall? 

I wonder if progress, unification, restoration and healing wouldn't come quicker if those of us who claim to be Biblically mature would take the lead in asking questions, listening to understand instead of respond, and just like Jesus did on the cross, be willing to go more than halfway to mend what is broken? 

An important and fair question for us as well as the people we lead: have I asked more questions to understand or have I jumped to conclusions too quickly? 

Then, looking through the lenses of my first two points here, were those conclusions Biblically founded and theologically driven?

Personally, before I felt confident in posting this article, I had a situation where I needed to call and ask some questions! Gotta practice what we preach, right?! It was a blessed conversation that didn’t change my opinions but most certainly helped me think and act more Christ-like with that person!

Pastor, as you train and shepherd your people to be passionate about what Scripture says yet at the same time have their default reaction to be curiosity, I pray that the Holy Spirit works through you in powerful ways to clear the fog created by society and social media. 

May we and the people we lead, even when we know something is off Biblically, take a humble, vulnerable posture of a curious learner which diffuses tension so effectively. 

I’ve learned that in my own life over and over again! 

Ok, fine, so if my previous three encouragements don't move the needle for your people, just go ahead and have them **label everyone they disagree with to be their ENEMY!** 

That statement probably made you perk up a bit! Is that really consistent with what I've been saying to this point? 

Is it consistent with Scripture?! Well, no...and yes. 

(Pastor, feel free to play this card with your church one of these days!)

> “You have heard that it was said, ‘Love your neighbor and hate your enemy.’  But I tell you, **love your enemies and pray for those who persecute you**, that you may be children of your Father in heaven. He causes his sun to rise on the evil and the good, and sends rain on the righteous and the unrighteous. If you love those who love you, what reward will you get? Are not even the tax collectors doing that? And if you greet only your own people, what are you doing more than others? Do not even pagans do that? Be perfect, therefore, as your heavenly Father is perfect.
> -Matthew 5:43-48

So, yea, if a follower of Jesus simply cannot figure this out then let's be grounded and driven by this passage where Jesus on the sermon on the mount turned the world upside down. 

If in their heart and mind a person cannot get over the conflict, whatever it is at that time, then go right ahead and consider the other side your enemy. 

Then, do what the Bible says which brings us right back to point #1, Be Biblically Grounded. 

Loving our enemies is tough. How do we even begin to do that? 
Step 2 in Jesus' command (yes it's a command), pray for them. 

Dr. Alvin Vandergriend in his book Love to Pray said, "You cannot pray for someone for five minutes without viewing them and loving them more like Jesus." 

If it comes to playing the 'enemy card' and if we are committed to being Biblically grounded and Theologically driven, then this love/pray strategy will be transformational starting first within you and then, I believe with my whole heart, in the conflict around you.

Pastor, you more than most anyone in your peoples’ lives are positioned to train this, model this and celebrate the fruit of this! I’ll continue to say it, I am praying for you and am with you!!

Finally, our mandate from the Lord is not to be right. We aren't commissioned to go and *convince* people of anything. We are called to love, to make disciples and one of the best ways to do that is to: 

## Live lives that adorn the Gospel in all things

A follower of Jesus who is Biblically grounded, theologically driven and loves their enemies enough to be curious to understand the world around them will actually attract people to the Gospel. Unfortunately under the banner of Jesus and/or Christianity there has been great hurt done on all sides, this conflict especially. But, I could include covid, recent elections, foreign wars, Super Bowl halftime shows and many others. Rather than adorning the Gospel far too many christians have done damage to its advancement, both online and in person. 

Maybe I should have led with this as point #1 because that is what this is for Jesus followers! Making the story of Jesus known and attractive to the world around us!

I recently spoke at a local Fellowship of Christian Athletes huddle not too far from my home. Since it was their Christmas party night I figured I'd speak out of my favorite Christmas passage, Titus chapter 2! I know, I know, that's not a common passage when thinking about Jesus' birth, but for me it ties together perfectly. In this chapter Paul is instructing Titus as he leads the church in Crete, to have the older men and women teach the younger men and women certain matters of how to conduct themselves in the society they lived in. While the specific teachings on conduct all make sense, I go and focus on the "why" he gives for living such a way.

vs. 5 - *so that no one will malign the word of God.*

vs. 8 - *so that those who oppose you may be ashamed because they have nothing bad to say about us.*

vs. 10 - *so that in every way they will make the teaching about God our Savior attractive.*

Verse 10 especially sums up the challenge I leave with you (pastor and all followers of Jesus) here. I often point to or at least refer to a Christmas tree and how at any other time of the year it would just be some old tree. But, when you put lights and ornaments on it, those adornments draw peoples' attention to it and it makes it something they want to look at longer. That is how our lives ought to be to a world that is on track to be lost for eternity! How we live, our conduct, should point people to Jesus! 

I also love and use Colossians 4:2-6:

> "Devote yourselves to prayer, being watchful and thankful. And pray for us, too, that God may open a door for our message, so that we may proclaim the mystery of Christ, for which I am in chains. Pray that I may proclaim it clearly, as I should. Be wise in the way you act toward outsiders; make the most of every opportunity. Let your conversation be always full of grace, seasoned with salt, so that you may know how to answer everyone."

While there is a lot to unpack here I want to pull out two things:

First is being wise in the way we act towards outsiders, those who do not yet have a saving relationship with Jesus. 

Same as in Titus 2, we need to be aware of our conduct because they are watching and we never know when an opportunity to show or share God's love may come up!

Second is this beautiful picture in verse 6, "conversation full of grace, seasoned with salt." You've heard the line, "You can lead a horse to water, but you can't make him drink!" Well, true. But, an old farmer once told me, "Well, you can salt his oats!!" He's so right!! I bet you that after eating those salty oats that the horse chugged gallons water when he got there!

I believe both statements are true. We cannot force anyone to surrender their lives to Jesus. But, we can live and speak in such a way that acts like salt to them. It makes the flavor of our words and lives desirable to them. They want to be around us. They want to listen to what we have to say. And, ultimately, they want to know what makes us different because they want that same flavor in their lives!

Pastor are the people in your church acting, speaking and reacting in a flavor that makes the world around them ‘thirsty’ for what is in them? Or is their ‘flavor’ one that is repelling the lost, or fellow believers for that matter, from the grace and restoration that comes through Jesus?

Back to Titus 2. As Paul encouraged Titus, so I encourage you, *For the grace of God has appeared that offers salvation to all people. It teaches us to say “No” to ungodliness and worldly passions, and to live self-controlled, upright and godly lives in this present age, while we wait for the blessed hope—the appearing of the glory of our great God and Savior, Jesus Christ, who gave Himself for us to redeem us from all wickedness and to purify for Himself a people that are his very own, eager to do what is good. These, then, are the things you should teach. Encourage and rebuke with all authority. Do not let anyone despise you.*

Vs. 11 - God’s grace appeared in Jesus coming for you and I, and, those we disagree with

Vs. 12 - Adorning the Gospel is a life that has renounced ungodly and worldly passions (fleshly, political, etc.)

Vs. 13 - We have hope and confidence that Jesus will come again

Vs. 14 - Adorning the Gospel is a life zealous to do good works!

Vs. 15 - You got this, pastor!

So, my friend, in view of God's mercy, I urge you to live a life worthy of the calling you have received, Biblically grounded, theologically driven, enemy lover and prayer, and as a curious learner who adorns the Gospel in all settings. 

And then lead your people to do/be the same! The Bible does not call us to be right. It calls us to, as an outflow of God's love that has transformed us, to reflect that love to all those around us. 

That will only be truly and fully done through the power of the Gospel which Jesus paid for and earned on the cross. No method or strategy of man, or even Biblical principles that fall short of including the ultimate salvation of the souls we see in front of us, will be effective. 

Father, Your Kingdom come, Your will be done here on Earth as it already is in Heaven.

amen
